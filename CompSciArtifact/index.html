Physics Simulations

So What Are They?

As the name suggests, Physics Simulations are simulations that attempt to replicate realistic physics. These simulations usually take an extremely long time to calculate and render, so a single second of the simulation could anywhere between a few minutes or multuple hours to process, depending on the scale of the simulation.


What Are They Used For?

Physics Simulations can be used for a multitude of real world purposes, including but not limited to:

  • Aerodynamics: An accurate aerodynamic simulation can help while designing new parts for planes, cars, or any other sort of object attempting to minimize drag or create lift.
  • Special Effects: Simulations could generate extremely realistic effects to replace potentially hazardous props when creating a film.
  • Mechanics: A good simulation of a machine could predict a quite few things about it, including:
    • How exactly it will function.
    • The strain placed on it's components.
    • Any potential errors that could occur.
  • Research: By simulating an environment and recreating it physically, scientists are able to test the accuracy of the math used in current physics or perhaps uncover new laws entirely!
  • A bit of good fun: No seriously, it's really cool to watch how a computer can mimic real life using only what math it was given. Add some nice shaders on it and it becomes almost impossible to tell it isn't real.

Innovations

Our ability to replicate real physics is improving at a very rapid pace, with people discovering new innovative solutions to problems nearly as fast as problems arise. One of my favorite examples of a massive leap in the field was with three-way coupling, where we could accurately simulate an object floating on water due to it's surface tension, something that was nearly impossible before.
